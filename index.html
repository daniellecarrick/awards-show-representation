Women are underrepresented at The Golden Globes

Of the 25 award categories at The Golden Globes, four of them are gender-neutral, including Best Director, Best Screenplay, Best Song and Best Score.

  • Since 1995, less than 12 percent of nominees in those categories have been women. Only 14 percent of nominated women end up taking home the award.
  • In the last 20 years, no woman has won for Best Director or Best Original Score
  • The Best Original Song category has had the highest percentage of female nominees (19%) and winners (14%).
  • While only 7% of nominees for The Best Original Screenplay category are women, 9% of the winners are.
  • Both Best Orignal Song and Best Original Screenplay boast a larger percentage of female nominee and winners but unlike Best Director, nominees tend to be writing teams, rather than individuals.
